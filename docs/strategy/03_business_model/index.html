    Business Model, Pricing Strategy & Financial Projections¶

    Agent Testing & Evaluation Platform ("AgentTest")¶

    Task #3 — Authored by biz-strategist

    1. Executive Summary¶

    AgentTest is an open-source-core, product-led-growth SaaS platform for testing, evaluating, and monitoring AI agents in CI/CD pipelines. The business follows a proven OSS→PLG→Enterprise funnel:

    1. Land via OSS — free CLI/SDK on GitHub drives developer adoption
    2. Expand via SaaS Cloud — usage-based self-serve unlocks persistent infra, team collaboration, and history
    3. Scale via Enterprise — SSO, RBAC, audit logs, SLAs, and on-prem unlock large org budgets

    5-Year Revenue Target: $18M ARR by Year 5 Funding Path: $1.5M Pre-seed → $6M Seed → $20M Series A Primary Beachhead: AI/ML teams at Series A–C product companies (20–500 engineers)

    2.2 Key Pricing Insights¶

    • Dev tools command $15–$89/seat-equivalent/month at team tier — well above generic SaaS
    • Usage-based pricing (Datadog, PostHog, CircleCI) dominates infra/observability tools — aligns cost with value
    • OSS + usage-based (PostHog model) is the highest-growth pattern: >90% of users stay free, but paid users spend heavily
    • Braintrust and Promptfoo obscure pricing → hiding pricing behind sales calls is a wedge opportunity (our transparent pricing wins developers)
    • Eval volume is the natural unit — evals/runs, replay storage, CI minutes are all sensible meters
    • Enterprise ACV for dev tools: $20k–$150k+/year (LaunchDarkly, Datadog patterns)

    3. Revenue Model & Pricing Tiers¶

    3.1 Pricing Philosophy¶

    "Free for individuals, affordable for startups, priced for value at enterprise."

    • Meter by eval runs (primary), not seats — aligns cost to actual agent testing activity
    • Seat-based floor at team tier — ensures baseline revenue predictability
    • Storage as secondary meter — replay traces, assertion logs, historical runs
    • Transparent, published pricing (no "contact sales" below $50k ACV)

    3.2 Pricing Tiers¶

    Tier 0: OSS / Self-Hosted (Free Forever)¶

    • Full CLI + SDK open source (MIT license)
    • Local eval runner, local results storage
    • No persistent cloud, no team features, no history beyond local
    • Target: Individual developers, hobbyists, students
    • Revenue: $0 — pure acquisition funnel

    Tier 1: Developer Cloud (Free)¶

    • Cloud dashboard, run history (30 days)
    • Up to 5,000 eval runs/month
    • 1 project, 1 user
    • Community support
    • Target: Individual developers, early adopters, OSS contributors
    • Revenue: $0 — conversion funnel entry

    Tier 2: Starter ($49/mo, billed annually = $39/mo)¶

    • 25,000 eval runs/month (overage: $2/1k runs)
    • 3 users / seats
    • Run history: 90 days + 10 GB replay storage
    • CI/CD integrations (GitHub Actions, CircleCI, GitLab CI)
    • Slack/email alerts
    • Target: Individual developers + small teams proving out agent testing
    • Estimated ARPU: $540/yr

    Tier 3: Team ($199/mo, billed annually = $169/mo)¶

    • 100,000 eval runs/month (overage: $1.50/1k runs)
    • Up to 15 seats
    • Run history: 1 year + 50 GB replay storage
    • Custom eval metrics, regression detection, A/B eval comparisons
    • Role-based access, shared dashboards
    • Priority support (48hr response)
    • Target: AI-focused product teams at Series A–B companies
    • Estimated ARPU: $2,400/yr

    Tier 4: Business ($599/mo, billed annually = $499/mo)¶

    • 500,000 eval runs/month (overage: $1/1k runs)
    • Up to 50 seats
    • Unlimited run history + 200 GB replay storage
    • Advanced analytics, cross-agent benchmarking, dataset versioning
    • SSO (SAML/OIDC), audit logs
    • Webhook integrations, API access
    • Dedicated onboarding, SLA 99.9%
    • Target: Mid-market, scale-ups with multiple agent products
    • Estimated ARPU: $7,200/yr

    Tier 5: Enterprise (Custom, $2,000–$20,000+/mo)¶

    • Unlimited eval runs / custom volume commitments
    • Unlimited seats
    • On-prem / VPC deployment option
    • Custom data retention + compliance (SOC 2, HIPAA, GDPR)
    • Dedicated CSM + SLA 99.99%
    • Volume discounts on replay storage
    • Custom eval framework integrations
    • Target: Large enterprises, regulated industries, AI-native companies
    • Estimated ARPU: $60,000–$200,000/yr

    3.3 Usage Metering Strategy¶

    Meter Unit Why
    Eval runs Primary Direct proxy for agent testing activity — scales with team growth
    Replay storage GB/mo Trace/log retention has real infra cost; tiered free allowance prevents abuse
    Seats Users Floor revenue; collaboration driver
    CI run minutes Minutes (enterprise) For on-prem or hybrid compute environments
    Dataset rows Rows (future) If we offer hosted ground-truth dataset management

    Overage pricing caps at $3/1k runs for Starter down to $0.75/1k runs at high Enterprise volume — keeps cost predictable while monetizing power users.

    4.3 OSS-to-SaaS Conversion Assumptions¶

    Industry benchmarks for OSS→paid: - 1–3% of active OSS users convert to any paid plan (conservative; typical for tools with strong self-host option) - PostHog reports <10% of users pay; most pay small amounts - Best case (if self-host is meaningfully inferior): 3–5%

    Our model assumptions: - OSS GitHub active users → Cloud free signup: 15–20% (strong CLI UX → cloud for persistence) - Free cloud → Starter paid conversion: 6–8% (generous free tier drives activation; PQL signals trigger upgrade) - Starter → Team upgrade (12-month): 20–25% (team growth natural expansion) - Team → Business upgrade: 15–20%

    6. Funding Strategy¶

    6.1 Funding Rounds¶

    Pre-seed: $1.5M (Month 0–3)¶

    • Target raise: $1.0M–$1.5M
    • Post-money valuation: $7M–$10M (5–10x ARR or milestone-based)
    • Use of funds: 2 founding engineers + founder runway for 18 months; OSS v1 launch
    • Milestones to unlock seed:
    • 500+ GitHub stars (credibility signal)
    • 10+ design partners committed to testing alpha
    • Working OSS CLI with 3+ integrations (LangGraph, CrewAI, OpenAI Agents)
    • Target investors: Pre-seed dev tools funds — Heavybit, Boldstart, ossfund, angels from Sentry/PostHog/Datadog

    Seed: $5M–$7M (Month 12–18)¶

    • Target raise: $5M–$7M
    • Post-money valuation: $25M–$40M (based on 2025 median seed SaaS ~$20M; premium for AI infra)
    • Use of funds: 5→11 team, SaaS Cloud v1 launch, first enterprise POCs, 18-month runway
    • Milestones to unlock Series A:
    • 3,000+ GitHub stars, 500+ weekly active CLI users
    • $500k ARR / 100+ paying customers
    • 2 enterprise pilots converting to paying ($80k+ ACV each)
    • NRR > 110% (existing customers expanding usage)
    • Target investors: Heavybit, Felicis (dev tools thesis), a16z (backed Promptfoo), General Catalyst, Unusual Ventures

    Series A: $18M–$25M (Month 30–36)¶

    • Target raise: $18M–$25M
    • Post-money valuation: $90M–$150M (based on 2025 Series A median ~$60M; premium for AI eval category)
    • Use of funds: Enterprise GTM motion, 20→30 team, on-prem deployment, compliance certifications (SOC 2)
    • Milestones to unlock Series B:
    • $3M–$5M ARR
    • 10+ enterprise contracts at $50k+ ACV
    • NRR > 120%
    • CAC payback < 18 months (enterprise)
    • Target investors: Insight Partners (backed Promptfoo), Redpoint, IVP, Bessemer, Scale VP (backed Galileo)

    7. Go-To-Market Strategy¶

    7.1 Phase 1: OSS Launch & Developer Awareness (Months 1–12)¶

    Goal: 1,000+ GitHub stars, 200+ weekly active users, 50 paying customers

    Channels: - GitHub + OSS Launch - Ship polished README with demo GIFs/videos, quick-start guide - HackerNews "Show HN" launch (target front page) - Dev.to, Lobsters, r/MachineLearning, r/LocalLLaMA posts - Content Marketing (SEO + thought leadership) - "How we test AI agents at [company]" case studies - Benchmark posts: "Which agent framework is most testable?" - LLM eval comparison posts (Braintrust vs Promptfoo vs AgentTest) - YouTube: 5-minute "eval your agent in CI/CD" tutorials - Developer Communities - Discord/Slack: LangChain, CrewAI, AutoGen, OpenAI developer communities - Conference talks: AI Engineer Summit, SREcon, KubeCon (MLOps track) - Framework Partnerships - Ship native integrations: LangGraph, CrewAI, Vercel AI SDK, Autogen, Semantic Kernel - Co-create content with framework maintainers - Design Partner Program - 10–20 early companies: 6 months free Business tier for feedback + case study rights

    Metrics targets (M12): - 1,000 GitHub stars - 500 weekly CLI users - 45 paying customers - $54k ARR

    7.2 Phase 2: PLG SaaS Growth (Months 12–30)¶

    Goal: Self-serve flywheel, $500k ARR, NRR > 110%

    Channels: - Product-Led Activation - In-product upgrade prompts triggered by usage limits (PQL signals) - Team invite virality: free seats unlock when you invite 2+ users - Usage-based email drip: "You're at 80% of your free eval quota" - Showcase: public eval leaderboards / shared benchmark results - Growth Marketing - SEO: target "how to test LLM agents", "agent evaluation CI/CD", "AI regression testing" - Paid (small budget): GitHub-sponsored posts, dev newsletter ads (TLDR, Bytes.dev, The Pragmatic Engineer) - Case studies: 3–5 published company stories with concrete metrics (eval coverage %, bugs caught) - Community Building - Launch Discord server for OSS community - Monthly eval office hours / webinars - AgentTest Eval Registry: shareable public eval suites (network effects) - Partnerships - CI/CD platforms: native GitHub Actions marketplace integration (top-featured) - Cloud marketplaces: AWS Marketplace, GCP Marketplace listing (procurement shortcut for enterprises) - AI platform integrations: Vercel, Replicate, Modal, Anyscale

    Metrics targets (M30): - 5,000+ GitHub stars - 250 paying customers - $517k ARR - NRR 110–115% - CAC payback < 9 months (self-serve tiers)

    7.3 Phase 3: Enterprise Motion (Months 30–60)¶

    Goal: Enterprise ACV + land-and-expand, $5M+ ARR

    Channels: - Outbound Sales Motion - ICP: VP/Director of AI Engineering at Series B+ companies, 50–500 engineer orgs - SDR-led outbound: LinkedIn + personalized email sequences referencing their public agent work - AE-led enterprise POC → full contract (60-90 day POC cycle) - Enterprise Features as Moat - SOC 2 Type II certification (required for procurement) - On-prem / VPC deployment option (critical for regulated industries: fintech, healthcare) - Advanced security: SSO, SCIM provisioning, audit logs - Custom SLAs + dedicated support - Channel Partnerships - System integrators: Accenture AI, Deloitte Digital (embed in enterprise AI delivery) - Consulting firms building agent products for clients - Conference Presence - Sponsor: AI Engineer World's Fair, AWS re:Invent, Gartner Data & AI

    Metrics targets (M60): - 18+ enterprise contracts ($60k+ ACV) - $8.5M ARR - NRR 120%+ - Enterprise ACV growing to $100k+ avg

    OSS-to-SaaS conversion lower than modeled (self-host wins) Medium Medium Make cloud meaningfully better: collaboration, history, CI integration — not just storage
